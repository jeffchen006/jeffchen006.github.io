I am a Ph.D. candidate at the University of Toronto luckily supervised by Prof. Fan Long. My current research focuses on program execution with zero-knowledge proofs, as well as program analysis and synthesis on smart contracts. I maintain a broad interest in programming languages, software engineering, security, and cryptography.

I also work as a part-time Research Engineer at Zircuit on zkrollups and sequencer-level security, where I'm working with Dr. Z-Dr. Martin Derka. and Dr. Jan Gorzny. Previously, I also worked at Quantstamp on smart contract auditing and automated vulnerability detection.

Earlier in my graduate studies, I was mentored by Prof. Umang Mathur in the PLMW program. Prior to that, I was advised by Prof. Xinyu Wang at the University of Michigan on several regular expression program synthesis projects, as well as by Prof. Paul A. Green on driving simulator projects.

Individual research project of EECS499

with Prof. Xinyu Wang

[report]

This project proposes an algorithm to synthesize the most probable sub-expressions of a ground-truth regular expression based on input–output examples and evaluates the effectiveness of subexpression queries.
